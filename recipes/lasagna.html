Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian comfort dish made by layering pasta sheets with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella. It's hearty, flavorful, and perfect for family dinners or gatherings.

The slow-cooked sauce combined with gooey cheese and al dente pasta creates a satisfying, savory experience in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (24 oz) jar marinara sauce
  • 1 (15 oz) container ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions; drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, sauté onions and garlic, then add ground beef and cook until browned.
  4. Add marinara sauce to the meat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, salt, and pepper.
  6. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
  7. Layer 3 noodles, half the ricotta mixture, a portion of the meat sauce, and mozzarella cheese. Repeat layers.
  8. Top with the final layer of noodles, remaining sauce, and Parmesan cheese.
  9. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  10. Remove foil and bake uncovered for another 15 minutes until the top is bubbly and golden.
  11. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.
